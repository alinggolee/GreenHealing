# Week 6 ｜Reflection & Meaning: From Bees to Life

From Bees to Life**

## Learning Goals

- reflect on the characteristics of solitary bees
- analyze the relationship between bees and the environment
- connect solitary bees to personal life and relationships
- express ideas through discussion and reflection

## O — Orientation

"Last week, you built a home for bees."
"Today, we ask a deeper question."
"What can bees teach us about life?"

### Teacher guidance:

"Watch and think."
"What kind of life do these bees have?"
"Is it easy—or difficult?"

## D — Delivery

"Let’s review key ideas."

Characteristics of Solitary Bees
live alone
build their own nest
prepare food for offspring
do not rely on a group

### Teacher guidance:

"They are independent."
"But they are not isolated."
"They are still connected to the environment."

Bees & Environment
"Solitary bees and plants depend on each other."
"No bees → fewer plants
No plants → no bees"
"This is a relationship."

## I — Interaction


### Activity 1: Guided Reflection

"Think about these questions."
(Pause between each)
What is special about solitary bees?
Which bee impressed you the most?
Why?

### Activity 2: Ecology Thinking

"Now think bigger."
What is the relationship between bees and nature?
What happens if bees disappear?

### Teacher guidance:

"Do not answer quickly."
"Think deeply."

### Activity 3: Personal Connection

"Now connect to your life."
"Are you more like a solitary bee
or a social bee?"

### Questions:

Do you like to work alone or with others?
Do you take care of others?
What kind of ‘home’ do you build in life?

## Sentence frames:

"I feel I am similar to…"
"Because in my life…"
"I think this means…"

### Activity 4: Life Meaning Discussion

"Let’s go deeper."
What can solitary bees teach us about parenting?
About independence?
About life challenges?

### Teacher guidance:

"There is no right answer."
"But there must be a real answer."

### Activity 5: Sharing

"Share your idea with your group."
"Then one person shares with the class."

## R — Recap

"Solitary bees are small."
"But their meaning is big."
"They teach us:"
responsibility
independence
connection
"You are part of this system."
