Seasonal Awareness: Solar Terms and Green Healing

## Learning Goals

> By the end of this week, students will be able to:

- understand the basic idea of solar terms and seasonal change.
- describe how seasons influence body, mood, and daily life.
- explain the connection between seasonal rhythm and wellbeing.
- observe and reflect on their own seasonal experience.
- begin thinking about how seasonal elements can be used in green healing activities.

## Content

This week focuses on seasonal awareness and how natural rhythms shape human life.
Solar terms are a traditional way to understand changes in nature across the year. They are not only about weather. They also reflect changes in:
temperature
light
plants
daily habits
body conditions
In green healing, seasonal awareness is important because the body and emotions often respond to environmental change. For example, some people feel more energetic in spring, more tired in summer, calmer in autumn, or slower in winter.
This means that healing is not always the same. It can change with the season.
In this week, the goal is not to memorize all solar terms. Instead, the focus is on noticing:
What is changing in the environment?
What is changing in my body and feelings?
How are these changes connected?
This awareness becomes the foundation for designing meaningful green healing experiences later in the course.

## ODIR

## O — Orientation

Take a moment to think about the current season.
What do you notice in the environment?
Is the air warmer or cooler?
Are there more insects, more sunlight, or different smells?
Do you feel more energetic, or more tired?
Sometimes we move too fast and forget to notice these changes.
But seasonal change is always happening around us—and also inside us.
So instead of asking, “What is the correct answer?”, we start with:
What do I feel in this season?

## D — Delivery

Solar terms help us understand that nature follows a rhythm.
This rhythm is not random. It moves in patterns across the year.
These patterns influence:
sleep and energy
appetite and body condition
emotions and mood
Green healing connects to this idea by asking:
How can we work with nature, instead of against it?
For example:
In hot weather, people may need cooling, slowing, and rest
In colder times, people may need warmth, support, and gentle activity
In times of change, people may need balance and adjustment
So green healing is not fixed. It is seasonal, flexible, and responsive.
This is also where observation becomes important. Before designing any activity, we need to understand:
the environment
the people
the timing
Seasonal awareness is the first step toward meaningful program design.

## I — Interaction

Think about your own seasonal experience.
Which season do you like the most? Why?
Which season makes you feel uncomfortable? Why?
What changes do you notice in your body or mood across seasons?
What do you usually do to adjust?
Now listen to others.
You may find that people respond to seasons differently.
This difference is important, because green healing must consider individual needs, not only general ideas.
Then try one more step:
If you design a simple activity for this season, what would you include?
Would it be quiet, active, indoor, outdoor, warm, or cooling?

## R — Recap

Seasonal change is not only something outside us.
It is also something we experience in our body and emotions.
Solar terms help us notice patterns in nature.
Green healing helps us connect these patterns to care and wellbeing.
So this week is about learning to observe:
the environment
the body
the connection between them
This awareness will support later learning, especially when you begin to design green healing activities for others.
