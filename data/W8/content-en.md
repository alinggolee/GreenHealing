# Week 8 ｜Empathy Map: Reflecting on Experience

## Learning Goals

- By the end of this session, students will be able to:
- understand the structure and purpose of an Empathy Map.
- reflect critically on their previous interaction with adolescent girls.
- distinguish between observation and assumption.
- collaboratively build a shared understanding.

## O — Orientation

Activity: Revisiting the Solitary Bee Experience
Students reflect on their previous visit.

Reflection Questions
What did you observe last week?
What surprised you?
What might you have missed?
What assumptions did you bring with you?

### Teacher Guidance

“Ask students to slow down and recall specific moments.”
“Encourage concrete examples, not general impressions.”
“Help them notice what they may have overlooked.”

## D — Delivery

What is an Empathy Map?
Students are introduced to the framework.

YouTube Learning
“Empathy Map Explained (Design Thinking)”
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QwF9a56WFWA

Learning Focus
Say / Do / Think / Feel
Pain / Gain
Difference between observation and interpretation

### Teacher Guidance

“Pause the video and ask:
Which part relates most to your experience last week?”
“Help students connect theory to their real interaction.”

## I — Interaction

Task 1: Build the Empathy Map (30 minutes)
Students work in groups.

Guiding Questions
Say & Do
What did the girls say?
What did they do?
Think & Feel
What might they be thinking?
What emotions did you sense?
Pain & Gain
What challenges might they face?
What seemed to bring them comfort?

### Teacher Guidance (during activity)

“Ask: Is this observation or assumption?”
“Encourage students to separate evidence from interpretation.”
“Remind them not to stereotype.”

Group Presentation (Student-led)
Each group presents their Empathy Map.

## Discussion Questions

What patterns did you notice?
Which part is most meaningful?

Teacher Feedback
“You are beginning to see complexity.”
“Different groups noticed different things—that is important.”

## R — Recap

Teacher Summary
“Today, you practiced turning experience into structured understanding.”

Reinforcing the Spirit of Empathy
“Empathy is not about being correct.”
“It is about staying open and curious.”

Professional Ethics Reminder
Professional Catching
Do not force others to speak.
Confidentiality & Anonymity
Protect identity using pseudonyms.

Teacher Closing
“Next week, you will turn this into a Persona.”
“You will refine your understanding with expert input.”
