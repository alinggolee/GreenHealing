# Week 12 ｜Visualizing Life Stories: From Narrative to Creative Expression

## Learning Goals

- By the end of this session, students will be able to:
- transform their adolescent narratives into visual forms.
- develop symbolic and abstract thinking.
- connect personal stories with natural metaphors.
- evaluate creative works with empathy and reflection.

## O — Orientation

Teacher Introduction
“Last week, you shared your stories.”
“This week, we will transform those stories into images.”

### Teacher Guidance

“Help students see this as a process of meaning-making.”
“This is not about artistic skill, but about expression.”

## D — Delivery

Topic: From Narrative to Symbol
Students learn:
Abstraction (simplifying meaning)
Symbolization (using images to represent ideas)
Emotional representation

Suggested Direction
Students are encouraged to use:
Animals (e.g., solitary bee)
Plants (e.g., seasonal herbs)
Natural elements

### Teacher Guidance

“Encourage students to connect symbols with their personal meaning.”
“Ask: Why this image? What does it represent?”

## I — Interaction

Task 1: Create a Visual Narrative Card
Students design their own “Life Narrative Card”

Guiding Questions
After abstraction, which animal or plant represents you? Why?
What part of your story does this image show?

### Teacher Guidance (during creation)

“Support students who feel unsure about creativity.”
“Reassure them: meaning is more important than appearance.”

Task 2: Peer Appreciation & Evaluation
Students observe each other’s work.

Reflection Questions
Which card touched you the most? Why?
What meaning did you see in that work?

### Teacher Guidance

“Encourage respectful and appreciative feedback.”
“Focus on interpretation, not judgment.”

## R — Recap

Teacher Summary
“You have transformed your story into symbols.”
“This is a deeper level of reflection.”

Final Teacher Message
“When we can express our story in different forms,
we understand it more deeply.”
