# Week 11 ｜Adolescent Narrative Sharing & Ethical Listening

## Learning Goals

- By the end of this session, students will be able to:
- share selected parts of their adolescent development narrative.
- practice ethical and empathetic listening.
- recognize emotional resonance in others’ stories.
- reflect on shared human experiences.

## O — Orientation

Topic: Revisiting Ethical Principles

Teacher Opening Message
“Today is a sensitive and important session.”
“You are sharing parts of your own life.”

Ethical Guidelines
You can choose what to share.
You do not need to share everything.
You can pass if you feel uncomfortable.

### Teacher Guidance

“Create a safe space before asking students to share.”
“Normalize vulnerability, but do not force it.”

## D — Delivery

Sharing Instructions
Students will:
Share a selected part of their adolescent narrative
Listen to others

### Teacher Guidance

“Remind students:
We are not evaluating stories—we are witnessing them.”

## I — Interaction

Task 1: Student Sharing
Students share selected parts of their narratives.

Task 2: Peer Listening
After each sharing:
Reflection Prompt
Which part touched you the most?
Why did it resonate with you?

### Teacher Guidance (during activity)

“Observe emotional safety in the room.”
“If needed, slow down the pace.”
“Affirm students’ courage in sharing.”

## R — Recap

Group Reflection
What did you learn from others’ stories?
What surprised you?

Teacher Summary
“Today, you practiced listening with empathy.”
“You learned that every story carries meaning.”

Final Teacher Message
“When we listen deeply, we connect as human beings.”
