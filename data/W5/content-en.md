Week 5: Solitary Bee Workshop
Understanding & Making**

## Learning Goals

- By the end of this session, students will be able to:
- understand basic knowledge of solitary bees
- identify differences between solitary bees and social bees
- explain the role of solitary bees in pollination
- build a simple solitary bee hotel
- describe key features of a bee hotel

## O — Orientation

"Today, we are going to meet a very special kind of bee."
"Before we start, let me ask you a question."
"When you hear the word ‘bee’, what do you think of?"
"Do you think of honey?
Or danger?"
(Pause)
"Most people only know honey bees or wasps."
"But today, we will meet a different kind of bee."
"A quiet bee.
A bee that lives alone."

## D — Delivery

"Let’s understand three types of bees."

Types of Bees
"First, social bees."
"They live together."
"They share work."
Examples:
honey bees
wasps
"They have roles: workers, queens, guards."

"Second, solitary bees."
"They live alone."
"They build their own nests."
"They take care of their babies by themselves."

"Important fact:
More than 80% of bees are solitary."

Life Cycle
"All bees go through similar stages:"
egg
larva
pupa
adult
"But solitary bees do something special."
"They prepare food for their babies,
close the nest,
and leave."

### Teacher guidance:

"They are like single parents."
"They prepare everything—
but the baby grows alone."

Solitary Bees & Environment
"Solitary bees are important pollinators."
"They help plants grow."
"They support our food system."
Examples (from your material):
fruits
vegetables
flowers

### Teacher guidance:

"When you eat fruit,
you are connected to bees."

## I — Interaction


### Activity 1: Observation & Identification

"Look at the images or materials."
"Try to answer:"
Is this a social bee or a solitary bee?
What clues do you see?

### Teacher guidance:

"Look at the body."
"Is it hairy?
Is it carrying pollen?"

### Activity 2: Build a Solitary Bee Hotel

"Now, we will build a home."
"Not for us—for bees."

Steps:
choose natural materials
prepare tubes (with closed ends)
check hole size (about 0.5–1 cm)
arrange structure

### Teacher guidance:

"This is not decoration."
"This is habitat design."
"Think:
Would a bee want to live here?"

### Activity 3: Understanding the Design

"After building, ask yourself:"
Is it safe from rain?
Is it too hot?
Is it facing the right direction?

### Teacher guidance:

"You are not just making something."
"You are thinking like nature."
