# Week 7 ｜Green Healing USR: First Meeting

Green Healing USR – First Meeting
Encounter, Placement, and Connection**

## Learning Goals

- By the end of this session, students will be able to:
- understand the partner school environment and build first connections
- introduce basic ideas of solitary bees to adolescent girls
- install solitary bee hotels in appropriate locations
- initiate open conversations about self, relationships, and environment
- use the Green Healing Triangle (self–others–environment) to guide dialogue

## O — Orientation

"Today is our first meeting."
"We are not here to teach."
"We are here to meet, to listen, and to connect."

"Remember this."
"You are not the expert."
"You are a bridge."

"When you meet the students,
don’t try to impress them."
"Try to understand them."


On-site Opening (First Encounter)
"Take a moment."
"Look at this place."

"How is this school different from yours?"

"Now, let’s say hello."
"Keep it simple."

"Hi, I’m __.
Nice to meet you."

## I — Interaction


### Activity 1: Talking About Bees(Led by University Students)

University students guide:
"Some bees live together.
Some bees live alone."

"Solitary bees live alone."
"They build their own homes."
"They prepare everything for their babies."

Then ask girls:
"Which kind of life do you think is easier?
Living alone or living together?"

Follow-up:
"There is no right answer."


### Activity 2: Installing Bee Hotels(Led by University Students)

University students say:
"Now, let’s find a place for the bee hotel."

Guide together:
Is it dry?
Is it safe from rain?
Is there sunlight?

"Let’s choose together."

After placing:
"This is now a place for life."

"How do you feel?"


**Activity 3: Green Healing Tea & Open Conversation
(Led by University Students)**
"Now let’s sit together."
"Take your drink."
"This is a relaxed space."

University students guide open dialogue:
"We are going to talk freely."
"You can say anything."

Open-ended Questions(核心設計)
You can choose and ask naturally:

"Solitary bees live alone."
"When you hear this, what do you think of?"

"Solitary bees make me think of loneliness."
"What do they make you think of?"

"Solitary bee mothers prepare everything for their babies."
"They build walls, layer by layer, to protect them."

"But one day, the baby must break through everything to leave."
"If they fail, they cannot survive."

"This reminds me of life."
"This part touched me the most."
"What part touched you?"


"Do you think being alone is strong or difficult?"

"Do you prefer to be alone or with others?"

"What kind of ‘home’ makes you feel safe?"


Green Healing Triangle(自然帶入)
University students gently connect:
"Let’s think about three things:"
yourself
other people
the environment

"How are these connected?"


### Activity 4: Closing Sharing(Led by University Students)

University students invite:
"Let’s share one sentence."

## Sentence frames:

"Today, I felt…"
"I was surprised by…"
"I learned that…"


## R — Recap

Teacher speaks at the end:
"Thank you for today."

"Today was not a class."
"It was a meeting."

"You met a new place,
new people,
and a new way to think."

"You also met yourself."

"Next time we meet will be in one month."
"In that time, the bee hotels will begin to change."
"And maybe—you will also change."

"I look forward to seeing you again."
